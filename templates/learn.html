How does it work?

Example: 2 x 3 = 6

You just add the 1st number (2), over and over again depending on the 2nd number (3).
In this case you add 2, three times,
like this: 2 + 2 + 2 = 6

Why learn the times tables?

The simple answer is that, it will help you a lot with life in the future. There are so many situations in everyday life where you’ll need to know the answer straight away, you might not have time to pull out a calculator. Think of it as your ABC’s but for Maths!
Luckily you have our site to help you out!


Are there any tricks to this?

Trick 1 – order doesn’t matter, look: 2 x 3 = 6 and 3 x 2 = 6

Trick 2 – anything times by 0 always equals 0.

Trick 3 – anything times by 1 always equals the other you’re multiplying it by.

Trick 4 – use PRACTICE MODE to learn the RAPID way.
